Common Questions About Residential Movers

How long does a residential move usually take? +
The time it takes for a residential move depends on the number of possessions and the distance to the location. Most local moves, such as those involving a single-family house, are completed in a single day. Long-distance moves typically take one to three days, depending on distance, route and logistics.
Can residential movers safely handle fragile items? +
Residential movers can safely handle fragile items using skilled packing methods and heavy-duty packing materials, along with professional equipment and proper loading techniques. Moving items such as art, antiques, glassware and jewelry often involve custom crating, bubble wrap, and padded moving blankets for maximum protection.
What happens if something is damaged during a residential move? +
Reputable residential movers typically offer various types of valuation coverage and will help you file a claim if any items are damaged. While damage is rare, valuation coverage protects you in case something unexpected happens during the move.
